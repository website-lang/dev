The Method

Regaining Agency Through Gravity.

Trauma and homelessness disconnect us from our bodies. Before we can navigate complex systems like housing or employment, we must first learn to navigate gravity.

We use Street Tai Chi as an "On-Ramp." It is a low-barrier, non-verbal way to regulate the nervous system. By moving together, we create a safe container where people can reclaim their identity—not as a "case number," but as a human being standing on solid ground.

  • 1. Co-Regulation We don't heal in isolation. We heal by syncing our movement with a safe community.
  • 2. Consistency Rain or shine. We prove worth by showing up, creating a reliable rhythm in a chaotic world.
  • 3. Dignity First No forms to fill out. No intake questions. You belong simply because you are here.

The Morning Flow

We keep it simple. We keep it safe. Here is exactly what to expect.

01

Arrive on Your Terms

9:30 AM — Drift in. There is no pressure to "perform." Grab a warm burrito and a coffee. Socialize or stay quiet—you choose your level of engagement.

02

Identity Check-in

A volunteer does a gentle attendance check. We ask for your name (or nickname) because acknowledging your identity is the first step of the practice.

03

Grounding (Balance)

We start with simple balance boards. It activates the body before the mind. It’s okay to hold onto a friend or a tree. We find stability together.

04

The Practice

10:00 AM — The group moves as one. Led by peers, the Tai Chi flow is simple, repetitive, and focused on weight shifts. This is about regulating the nervous system, not perfect form.

05

Closing Circle

We reclaim our voices. We end with a short learning circle where anyone can share a motivation, an announcement, or a word of encouragement.

06

Support & Handoff

We close with tangible support (essential engagement items) and connection. This is often where "Pathway Connectors" help link participants to other services.
