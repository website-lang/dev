"I Just Needed 10 Minutes."

David didn't come for the Tai Chi. He came for the coffee. For three weeks, he stood outside the circle, arms crossed, watching. He was navigating severe PTSD and the chaos of the shelter system.

Then, one Tuesday, he stepped in. He didn't speak. He just followed the movement. "It was the first time in five years my brain stopped screaming," he later told us. That 10 minutes of silence was the beginning of his transition to housing.
